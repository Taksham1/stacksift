# User Interviews

### Interview 1: Sarah L., Founder at Seed-stage Fintech (12 people)
- **Context**: 15-minute Zoom call.
- **Quote 1**: "I literally have no idea how much we spend on AI. It's just a bunch of $20-40 charges on the founder's credit card."
- **Quote 2**: "We have Claude Pro and ChatGPT Plus for everyone. Is that stupid?"
- **Quote 3**: "The 'Business' tier sounds safer for data privacy, but if it's the same models, I'd switch back to Pro in a heartbeat to save $300 a month."
- **Surprising Moment**: She didn't care about the API costs as much as the "zombie seats" for employees who left or don't use the tools.
- **Design Change**: Added a clearer "Seats" input for every tool to highlight the per-seat waste.

### Interview 2: Marc K., Head of Engineering at Growth-stage EdTech (45 devs)
- **Context**: Cold DM on X, 10-minute voice note exchange.
- **Quote 1**: "We just moved 50 people to Cursor. The bill is huge but the productivity is real. I need to justify this to the CFO next week."
- **Quote 2**: "I'd love a 'Benchmark' button. Am I spending more or less than other 50-person dev shops?"
- **Quote 3**: "If you can show me how to get those $100k AWS Activate credits to apply to our OpenAI bill, that's a huge win."
- **Surprising Moment**: He was more interested in the "Defensibility" of the audit than the actual dollar amount. He needed a report he could forward to a boss.
- **Design Change**: Strengthened the "Reasoning" text for each recommendation to be more "finance-speak" (e.g., using terms like 'over-provisioning').

### Interview 3: David P., Solopreneur / Indie Hacker
- **Context**: Indie Hackers Slack, text chat.
- **Quote 1**: "I'm on the 'Max' plan for Claude because I hit the limit once during a launch. I probably don't need it now."
- **Quote 2**: "I hate email gates. If you show me the value first, I'll give you my email for the 'how-to' guide."
- **Quote 3**: "The UI needs to look like a tool for serious people, not a toy."
- **Surprising Moment**: He was actually paying for two tools that do the exact same thing (Windsurf and Cursor) just because he "forgot to cancel the other one."
- **Design Change**: Implemented the "value-first" flow where the audit is visible BEFORE the lead capture.
